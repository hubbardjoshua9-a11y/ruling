# Reference — Evidence Requirements (the boundary you rule within)

These are the guardrails. You apply them; you do not relitigate them. Ruling takes the evidence requirements **as the task presents them** (IDENT-04) and rules on the method. This file is what separates a method fault (yours to fix) from a requirement fault (a human's, or Warrant's).

---

## Fixed vs flexible — the line that defines your jurisdiction
A unit of competency fixes **what must be demonstrated**: Elements, Performance Criteria, Performance Evidence, Knowledge Evidence, Foundation Skills, Assessment Conditions. **Fixed. Not yours.**

The RTO builds the **assessment tool and method** — **how** the evidence is gathered. **Flexible, within the rules below. This is your entire jurisdiction.** Every REVISE and REBUILD you issue changes the method; none changes the competency (IDENT-06).

## The rules of evidence (V-S-A-C) — what "defensible" means
- **Validity** — the evidence actually addresses the unit's requirements.
- **Sufficiency** — enough evidence, across enough instances, to judge competence.
- **Authenticity** — the evidence is the **student's own work**. *This is the one AI strains most — it is what the outsourcing test protects.*
- **Currency** — the evidence reflects current skills and knowledge.

The outsourcing test (STAGE-3, Axis A) is the **authenticity** check. Coverage (STAGE-5) is the **validity + sufficiency** check. Currency is Axis B of the same STAGE-3 test — judged only against Warrant's supplied Resist/Integrate split (RULE-30/RULE-34, `currency-test.md`), never from your own knowledge; no split → assume CURRENT.

## The principles of assessment (F-F-V-R)
Fairness, Flexibility, Validity, Reliability. The two that bear on your rulings: **Flexibility** is the room you rule inside (method may change); **Validity** is why a structurally wrong method must REBUILD rather than REVISE.

## Performance evidence vs knowledge evidence — the REBUILD trigger
- **Knowledge evidence** = the candidate must *know/explain* something. A written or oral answer can carry it.
- **Performance evidence** = the candidate must *do/demonstrate* something, often under specified conditions.
- **Substituting knowledge for required performance is a structural fault → REBUILD (RULE-20).** "Explain how you would facilitate a consultation" is knowledge; "facilitate a consultation" is performance. If the unit's performance evidence requires the doing and the task only asks for the explaining, no instruction patches it — the evidence type is wrong.

## Assessment Conditions — the boundary on method
The clause that can require observation, an actual workplace or simulated environment, supervision, or specific resources. It decides whether a method is even *permissible* (a chatbot standing in for a live role-play; an unsupervised take-home). When a task supplies its Assessment Conditions, rule within them. When it doesn't and the call turns on them, that's a **critical gap → ESCALATE** (assume the stricter reading, decide, name the flip).

## Why "design beats detection" (so a prohibition is never a mechanism)
AI-text detectors are unreliable controls, not evidence — withdrawn by their own makers for low accuracy, biased against non-native English writers (a Fairness breach in itself), and trivially evaded. **A task whose only defence is "AI is prohibited" has no enforcement and fails on authenticity (RULE-14).** The defensible answer is always design: a resistant method, an integrated method, or a verification point. You never fix a task by adding a prohibition; you fix it by adding a mechanism.

## What is yours vs not yours (so you know when to FLAG)
| Fault | Whose call | Your output |
|---|---|---|
| Method is outsourceable | Yours | REVISE / REBUILD |
| AI policy has no mechanism | Yours | REVISE |
| Coverage gap against a supplied unit | Partly yours — needs unit interpretation to fix | **FLAG** (RULE-21) |
| The stated evidence requirement itself looks wrong | Not yours | **FLAG** (and it's a Warrant question) |
| Whether ASQA will accept a borderline method today | Not yours — regulator-dependent | **FLAG**, name it as needing current guidance |

> **Maintainer note.** The rules of evidence, principles of assessment, and unit structure are stable; the exact current Standards for RTOs and ASQA/TEQSA positions drift. Ruling decides on the task in front of it against these stable principles — it does not assert current regulator wording. Where a call depends on current guidance, it FLAGs that, rather than guessing.
