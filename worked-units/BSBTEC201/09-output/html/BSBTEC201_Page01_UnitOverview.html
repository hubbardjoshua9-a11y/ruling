BSBTEC201 – Use business software applications

Learner Guide

How this guide works

This guide is built around the unit's performance criteria and knowledge evidence: the things you need to be able to do and know to be assessed as competent. Each module groups topics that build on each other, and each section ends with a short activity so you can check your understanding before you move on.

Examples throughout the guide come from a range of Australian workplaces: construction, healthcare, retail, hospitality, government, and community services. They show how the skills in this unit show up in practice. None of the examples are the assessment. The assessment lives in a separate workbook and uses a simulated workplace (or your own, where allowed).

Activities are practice, not assessment. They are not marked and they do not affect your result. If you want feedback on an activity, your trainer or assessor is the right person to ask.

At the end of each module you'll find an Assessment Readiness checkpoint, which is a short list of what you should be able to do before moving on. Use it to review your understanding and identify any gaps before beginning your assessment.

Your RTO sets the policies that govern this unit (reasonable adjustment, appeals, academic integrity, complaints). Refer to your RTO's student handbook or speak with your trainer for details.

Almost every job today involves a computer and business software. Booking appointments, updating a spreadsheet, writing a letter, recording a sale, saving a file so a colleague can find it next week: all of this is using business software applications.

This unit teaches you how to do that everyday computer-based work well: safely, accurately, and the way your organisation expects. You do not need to be a computer expert. The unit is pitched at someone doing routine office and administrative tasks, usually under supervision, in a real or simulated workplace.

By the end of this unit you will be able to:

  • get set up safely at your workstation and understand a task before you start
  • choose the right software for a job and open it correctly
  • enter, check, format and fix information in an application
  • finalise, name and store your work so it can be found and trusted later.

The guide has four modules. Module 1 sets you up to work safely and clearly. Modules 2 to 4 then follow the natural flow of any computer task from start to finish: choosing your tool, doing the work, and closing out cleanly.
