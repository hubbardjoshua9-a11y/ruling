3.3 – Formatting so it is fit for purpose

Formatting makes information clear, consistent and professional. Use each application's formatting features appropriately for the task, and use your organisation's template or house style where one exists, so your work matches everything else that goes out.

  • In a word processor: use headings, bold, bullet or numbered lists, and alignment to make a document easy to read. Apply the organisation's template rather than inventing your own look.
  • In a spreadsheet: set sensible column widths, format numbers as currency or dates where needed, add borders or shading to separate sections, and use simple totals.
  • In a presentation: keep a consistent slide layout and a font size big enough to read from across a room.

Good formatting is not decoration. A well-formatted run-sheet or price list is faster and safer for the next person to use.

Examples across Australian workplaces
    • A hotel or function venue administrator formats an event run-sheet with clear headings and times so floor staff can read it at a glance during service.
    • A government office applies the department's document template to a report so it looks consistent with everything else the department publishes.
    • A trade supplies business formats a quote in its standard template, with the logo, totals and GST line in the expected places.
