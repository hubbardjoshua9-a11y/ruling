## Unit Summary

**Elements and Performance Criteria**
1. Select and prepare to use technology (1.1 identify task purpose, audience, format, presentation and clarify; 1.2 select relevant technology and software; 1.3 adjust workspace, furniture and equipment for own ergonomics).
2. Input and process information or data (2.1 identify and open application; 2.2 enter information or data; 2.3 check and amend; 2.4 format using application functions; 2.5 use help functions for simple issues).
3. Finalise and store document (3.1 review and edit, prepare for storage; 3.2 name and store document and exit application).

**Performance Evidence:** select and use at least three business software applications on two occasions each; select and use technology safely and according to organisational requirements; identify and address faults according to requirements.

**Knowledge Evidence:** the organisation's work health and safety requirements relevant to own role; the organisation's requirements for file naming and storage; applications used for organising electronic information and data.

**Foundation Skills:** Reading (interpret information from familiar sources to determine task requirements); Writing (produce and amend files; complete documentation using organisational formats).

### 1.1 About This Assessment

This task assesses your ability to **select and use business software safely and correctly to complete a real administrative job from start to finish**.

> It focuses on **working through a routine office task using several applications**, including:
> - setting up your workstation safely and understanding the task
> - choosing and using the right applications to prepare and check work
> - finding and fixing a simple fault, then naming, storing and sending your files correctly

No further task is required after this one. Your underpinning knowledge is assessed separately in Assessment 1 of 2 (Knowledge Questions).

*Step 3B — Verify the judgment is yours (completed unaided, at submission).*

Step 3A can be produced with an AI tool, so on its own it does not confirm the judgment is your own. **Step 3B is a separate, locked task** that confirms it. Here is exactly how it runs:

1. **Finish Step 3A first.** Do all of Step 3A in your own time, with AI, and submit it. Step 3B stays locked until you do — you cannot see it in advance.
2. **Submitting Step 3A unlocks Step 3B and starts a 15-minute timer.** Only at this point does the task reveal **one unseen change to the brief** — for example a changed start date or division, or a fourth new starter added. You are seeing it for the first time.
3. **Within the 15 minutes, record one take.** A single, unbroken **screen-and-voice recording (about 2–3 minutes), unaided — no re-prompting an AI tool.** On camera you: (a) make the change to your memo live; (b) say what you changed and why; and (c) name one decision in this memo you would **not** hand to an AI tool, and why.
4. **Upload the recording to finish.** That is your submission for Step 3B. There is no live session to book.
