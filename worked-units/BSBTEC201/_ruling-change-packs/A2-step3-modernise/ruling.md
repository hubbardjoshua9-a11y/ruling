  the current competency is directing an AI draft and quality-checking it, not
  manual original production. The task assesses a pre-AI workflow (currency OBSOLETE).
FIX [modernise]: convert Step 3 to an AI-integrated method (RULE-31). Note the
  two axes interact here: the currency fix re-opens an authenticity exposure, and
  the operator closes it in the same change.
  STEP 3A — judgment triad (RULE-22/24): use an AI tool to draft; record the prompt;
    identify ≥2 problems against brief/house-style; correct each and justify
    (Template T2a). Competency assessed becomes governing the tool.
  STEP 3B — verification anchor (RULE-30 currency stays the split's; method is the
    operator's jurisdiction, IDENT-06). Step 3A as a take-home is itself fully
    outsourceable — a student can run the whole triad through one AI session, so on
    its own it scores outsourcing MEDIUM. Rather than ship that, Ruling attaches a
    minimum-sufficient anchor: at submission the assessor releases ONE unseen change
    to the brief; the student records a 2–3 min unaided, one-take screen+voice
    response adapting the memo and naming one decision they would not hand to AI
    (Template T2b). Async, batched review — online-native, no scheduling. The unseen
    variation cannot be pre-generated, so this anchor compensates Step 3A's
    outsourceability and pulls the step to LOW (outsourcing-test.md §Compensation).

  jurisdiction (IDENT-06) to pair direct-and-document with the verification anchor.
  Operator, not transcriber.
